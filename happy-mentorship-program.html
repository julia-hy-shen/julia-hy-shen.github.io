Happy Mentorship School

Design work for a private mentorship program run by Yugina Yun. Made with Adobe Illustrator, June 2020.

BACKGROUND

Yugina commissioned me to create a logo and announcement graphic for her mentorship program, Happy Mentorship School, in June 2020.

CONTEXT

Happy Mentorship School is an application-based mentorship program, open to people in any industry of work. Yugina gave me a few design guidelines, listed below:

  • Imagery should reference Yugina’s personal brand, “Happy Yugina”
  • Use round shapes to communicate friendliness
  • Colors should be a gradient, using either blue/purple or peach/pink

First logo drafts.

PROCESS

Above are my early explorations of the logo.

  • These designs imitate the original Happy Yugina logo, viewable here.
  • I incorporated fish because Yugina loves marine biology, and often uses the subject in her personal brand and content.
  • In our first conversations, Yugina mentioned that the image of clasping hands might be a good visual fit for the program. Based on that, I created the rightmost designs.

Yugina selected the fish logo for further exploration, and suggested that the leading fish should be larger to reference the purpose of the program.

Further development of the fish design.

Yugina ultimately selected the fish bowl design (first from the left). I proceeded with refining the design's details.

The final logo, with a wordmark version. I hand-drew the seahorse-shaped "S" to continue the marine life theme.

OUTCOMES

I created several versions of the final design (including the wordmark displayed above) to be used on different types of graphics. Additionally, I created an announcement graphic for Twitter and Discord, viewable below.

IMPACT

To be announced! Happy Mentorship School is launching soon; you can learn more here.

REFLECTIONS

This was my first time designing a logo for a real product. The journey from initial conversation to final design was super fun, and I loved playing with the creative freedom that Yugina gave me. This was also my first time using Adobe Illustrator for the bulk of a project, and I gained a lot of confidence in creating custom designs from scratch.
