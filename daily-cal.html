Telling stories about esports, 2018-2020.

TEAM

Daily Cal Sports.

MY ROLE

As the paper’s first League of Legends beat reporter, I am responsible for covering the UC Berkeley DI League of Legends team. I also write about professional esports teams, tournaments, and personalities.
BACKGROUND

The Daily Californian is an independent, student-run newspaper that covers both the UC Berkeley campus and the city of Berkeley. I joined the Daily Cal in January 2018 as a founding member of its esports department, the first at any college newspaper.

GOALS
  1. Raise awareness of UC Berkeley’s collegiate esports teams and their accomplishments.
  2. Cover professional esports stories from a unique, student-oriented perspective.
OUTCOMES
  1. Wrote 42 news stories for both print and digital publishing, reaching an average of 350 readers per article.

Me and a coworker at the Golden State Warriors office for an interview about their esports team.

CONTEXT

Collegiate esports is frequently overlooked. It’s difficult to find consistent coverage of esports teams at any school, despite the fact that they regularly compete just like any other sports team. One of my goals in joining the Daily Cal was to bring recognition to collegiate esports players and their accomplishments.

As the Daily Cal’s League of Legends beat reporter, I write weekly articles about the Cal DI League of Legends team during their competitive season. During the collegiate offseason, I write about professional esports. For those stories, I’ve interviewed several League of Legends Championship Series personalities and players, including Sam “Kobe” Hartman-Kenzler, Samson “Lourlo” Jackson, Ovilee May, and Eugene "Pobelter" Park.

IMPACT

To date, I’ve published 42 stories at the Daily Cal, reaching an average of 350 readers per article. I’ve also been featured in print three times, which is pretty neat.

REFLECTIONS

Being a part of the Daily Cal has been immensely beneficial for my writing skills. The sports department in particular has pushed me to get creative and take more risks in the process of creating a story that I’m proud of.

I'm super grateful for the opportunities that the Daily Cal has given me. Being a staff writer has opened the door for me to create so much unique content (from interviews with professional League of Legends players to backstage videos at esports tournaments), and I'll never take that for granted.
