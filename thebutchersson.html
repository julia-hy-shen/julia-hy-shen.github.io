Website redesign for an independently owned restaurant in Berkeley, CA.

The restaurant website, before and after our redesign.

BACKGROUND

I found this pro-bono project through Outhrive, a non-profit that pairs student consultant teams with small businesses.

The Butcher’s Son is a popular vegan restaurant in Berkeley. At the start of quarantine, the restaurant was unprepared to transition to online orders, and saw a large drop in sales as a result of their outdated website structure. My team was tasked with completely updating their website’s content, design, and user experience.

GOALS
  1. Update website to better reflect the restaurant’s current branding, as well as modern design standards.
  2. Drive website usage and boost the visibility of important information (ex. COVID-19 policies, online order process, menu items) through improved user experience.
OUTCOMES
  1. Increased online traffic by 120%. The website originally received 5,000 visitors per month; following our update, the website now receives 11,000 visitors per month.

Our project timeline.

CONTEXT

Our task was to complete a brand refresh and UI/UX overhaul on the restaurant’s website to attract more customers during the COVID-19 quarantine. My team coordinated with the restaurant owners to concept and implement a redesign that they loved. I also worked with restaurant staff to edit all website copy and reduce information overload.

This entire project was completed over a period of one month (July 14 - August 14, 2020).

IDEATION & DESIGN PILLARS

First, we studied the existing website and identified the highest-priority changes.

Based on our observations, we established two main design pillars:

  1. Simplicity
    The original website was overloaded with buttons and pages to visit, which was confusing for potential customers. We wanted to reduce the clutter and make it easier for users to place orders.

  2. Unique visual identity
    The original website was created from a default Squarespace template, and had no specific identity. We wanted the new design to be visually distinct, and better reflect the restaurant's mission of serving all-vegan food.

Moodboard by Liz Fu. We decided on a neutral color palette with deep browns to reflect a natural, comforting image.

WIREFRAMING

With our design goals set, our team outlined an overall structure for the website. We each independently designed an information architecture diagram, then created a final version together.

Our final information architecture diagram.

PROTOTYPING

I was tasked with creating our mid-fidelity prototype from the designs that our team had discussed. Based on my initial design, we created several ideations and ultimately selected one to present to the store owners.

Left to right: the original website, the mid-fidelity prototype I created, and our final hi-fidelity prototype. Click to view larger. Note that these designs use the website's original, unedited copy.

OUTCOME

Our redesign significantly boosted traffic to The Butcher’s Son’s website. After our site went live in August, the number of monthly visitors doubled. Visitor numbers have remained constant through November 2020 (the time of writing), showing the lasting impact of our project.
