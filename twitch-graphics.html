Twitch Stream Graphics

Made with Adobe Photoshop, May 2020.

BACKGROUND

One of my friends, Patrick, recently started streaming on Twitch under the username “LegendAchiever9.” I designed graphics for his stream and Twitch profile. Patrick is a variety streamer, and he wanted his graphics to show off his favorite games.

I’m open for more work like this; if you’re interested, email me at julia.shen[at]berkeley[dot]edu or hit me up on Twitter.

OUTCOMES

Banner image.

Patrick plays top lane for UC Berkeley’s top-ranked competitive League of Legends team, and his favorite champion is Gangplank, an orange-eating pirate. I incorporated Gangplank’s in-game splash art into Patrick’s Twitch banner, which is displayed at the top of his profile.

"Offline" screen.

This “offline” screen is displayed whenever someone visits Patrick’s profile while he’s not streaming. The design is based on the UI of Hollow Knight, Patrick’s favorite game of all time. The background art is a screenshot of Hollow Knight’s "Mantis Lords" boss fight.

"Stream starting soon" screen.

Twitch streamers use “stream starting soon” screens to let their audience know that they’ll be online soon. Patrick is a huge Star Wars fan, so this design is based on the first few seconds of the Star Wars opening crawl.

"About" tab.

"FAQ" tab.

"Twitter" tab.

Many streamers use small images to divide their stream information into sections. Game references are as follows:

  1. “About me” tab - Hollow Knight. (The circular objects are “Charms” from the game, which players can mix and match to achieve different effects. The three Charms pictured make up Patrick’s favorite build.)
  2. “FAQ” tab - Ori and the Blind Forest.
  3. “Twitter” tab - A Short Hike.
REFLECTIONS

Creating some more personalized designs was a lot of fun, and imitating the visual styles of Patrick’s favorite games was an interesting challenge. You can view all of these designs live on the LegendAchiever9 Twitch profile.
